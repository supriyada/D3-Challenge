Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty

The scatter plot reveals the visual outcome of impact of poverty on healthcare on all 51 states in U.S. With the increase in the rate of poverty, the rate of lack in healthcare also increases. The maximum poverty rate is observed at 21.5% in state, Missisipi. The minimum poverty rate is observed at 9.2% in state, New Hampshire.

Similarly, Texas has the highest rate of lack in healthcare at 24.9% and Massachusetts at 4.6% enjoys most from healthcare benefits.

New Jersey has the poverty rate of 11.1% and lack of healthcare rate at 12.5%. So in overall it picks fourth position while correlating health risk and poverty in U.S. States.
