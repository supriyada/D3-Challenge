Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and other factors

The graph reveals the visual outcome of impact of various factors like poverty, income, age, obesity and somking influencing healthcare in all 51 states in U.S.

Poverty Vs Healthcare:With the increase in the rate of poverty, the rate of lack in healthcare also increases. The maximum poverty rate is observed at 21.5% in state, Missisipi. The minimum poverty rate is observed at 9.2% in state, New Hampshire.Similarly, Texas has the highest rate of lack in healthcare at 24.9% and Massachusetts at 4.6% enjoys most from healthcare benefits.

Income Vs Healthcare:As the median value of the household income decreases we could see an increase in the lack of healthcare. Maryland has the median income of $73,791, being the highest and Missisipi with median income of $39,680 which is the lowest. We also observed Missisipi has the highest poverty rate. Less income leads to poverty and thereby people are struggling to get proper healthcare.

Age Vs Obesity/Smokers: The rate of obesity & smokers in most of the states falls within the age range between 32 and 42 years. Utah has the obesity rate as low as 25.7% which is minimum as compared to Maine with rate 28.2% which is maximum. The same pattern is observed with smokers rate as well. Utah has the smokers rate as low as 9.7% which is minimum as compared to Maine with rate 19.3% which is maximum

The factors are all interrelated in some way or other and are found to be influencing the health of the individuals in the country!
